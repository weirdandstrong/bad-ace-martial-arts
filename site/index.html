What is Shou Shu?

The fighting way
of the beast.

Shou Shu is a Chinese martial art built on the movements of seven animals. Students learn to pair styles — the tiger's ferocious power with the crane's elegant precision — so a smaller defender can beat a bigger, stronger attacker. It isn't fighting for trophies. It's self-defense for life.

And you don't just learn the seven beasts — you earn them, one degree at a time:

Bear

1st Degree · Power

Tiger

2nd Degree · Ferocity

Mongoose

3rd Degree · Speed

Crane

4th Degree · Balance

Mantis

5th Degree · Control

Cobra

6th Degree · Precision

Dragon

7th Degree · Mastery

“We don't break bricks, because bricks don't fight back.”

— Shifu Alexis Potter, Head Instructor

Women's self-defense

This studio exists because of one women's self-defense class.

Shifu Alexis walked into a women's self-defense class as a student and walked out hooked on the feeling of empowerment. Now she teaches it — techniques built on natural movements anyone can do, no athletic background required.

Answering a phone is the same motion that breaks a wrist grab. Opening a cupboard is a back-knuckle strike. You already have the movements. We teach you to use them.

Questions parents ask

Before your first class.

What age can my child start?

Little Dragons starts at age 4. We meet kids where they are — early classes focus on listening, motor skills, stranger awareness, and confidence, not combat.

What should we wear to a first class?

Comfortable athletic clothes and a water bottle. No uniform or gear needed until you decide to join.

Is the training safe? Is there sparring?

Safety runs every class. Students learn control first, and any partner work is supervised, progressive, and matched to age and experience.

How much does it cost?

Come take your free class first — then we'll walk you through membership options in person and find what fits your family. No pressure, no contracts sprung on you.
